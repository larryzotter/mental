Stress can be classified into either long or short term.

Another way to think about stressors is to see if the stressor will happen at a certain point in the future, or if it is ongoing and spread out over a certain duration.

Long term (Chronic) stress

Most people will experience some type of long term stress in their life. This type of stress involves something more substantial than short term stress and can be damaging.

Short Term (Acute) Stress

The most common type of stress experienced is short term stress. Short term stress is harmless in small amounts; however, too much short term stress can cause feelings of exhaustion, worry and tension.

The section below shows examples of long and short term stressors, with some general tips to deal with them.

Long-term Short-term

Academic

Unhealthy competition with classmates and peers
Being jealous when others succeed.
High expectations on oneself and feeling demoralized when one does not succeed

Tips

Act gracefully. You can’t guarantee that you will always succeed, but you can take charge of how you feel.
Focus on showing good character.
Be proud of others’ efforts and your own, no matter who comes out on top.
Create a vision/goals board and work towards your goals- do not feel disheartened if you fail at times, keep trying, change your perspective and never give up.
See a professional/counsellor/therapist if you do not feel better.

Personal

Balancing social life vis-a-vis family and school
Managing financial burden
High expectations on oneself
Being overcommitted

Tips

Choose to spend time with resilient and positive people
Improve your planning- start making daily check lists- take action by organizing your weekly activities
Get a Job that Doesn't Stress You out. Make a budget plan and stick to it
Learn relaxation techniques/ practice yoga/ meditation
Pay attention to your diet, exercise and sleep
Count your daily blessings by unleashing gratitude
See a professional/counsellor/therapist if you do not feel better

Internship/Work

Increasing demands from work/ boss
Long hours at work

Tips

Identify the aspects of the situation you can control and aspect you can’t.
Gently confront your boss to set mutual expectations and priorities.
Eat right and sleep well.
See a professional/counsellor/therapist if you do not feel better

Academic

Exam anxiety before the start of the finals.
Impending deadlines of assignments and projects.

Tips

Manage time wisely- Get organized (draw up a time-table if it helps)
Create a good study environment- find a good study buddy who is encouraging.
Learn study skills from peers, classmates or professors.
Seek help from classmates/ peers/ professors if you do not understand any concept or skills.
Get enough sleep

Personal

Building new friendships
New environment/culture

Tips

Join a Co-curricular activity or volunteer for a cause

Internship/Work

New environment
Tight deadlines

Tips

Spend a few minutes to do some deep breathing if you feel overwhelmed or tensed.
If you’re not working on an important project, handle your small tasks the second they come in – and figure out an efficient system to complete the tasks quickly.

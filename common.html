Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety disorders such as Generalised anxiety disorder, panic disorders (attacks) and Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD), depression, and eating disorders are increasingly prevalent in youth.

Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety disorders are the most common types of mental health problems.
The individual has a severe fear or anxiety, which is linked to certain objects or situations.
Most people with an anxiety disorder will try to avoid exposure to whatever triggers their anxiety.

Some examples include:

Generalised anxiety disorder
Panic disorder/attack
Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD)

Distress

also known as bad or negative stress

Causes anxiety or concern

Is perceived as outside of our coping abilities

Feels unpleasant

Decreases performance

Can lead to mental and physical problems

A little bit of stress is not bad

Positive stress could give you energy, motivation and spark creativity; Also makes you more alert and helps you perform better in certain situations.

Stress is only beneficial if it is short-lived.

Excessive or prolonged stress can lead to illnesses such as heart disease and mental health problems such as anxiety and depression.
